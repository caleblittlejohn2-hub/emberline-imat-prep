Can you explain the Henderson-Hasselbalch equation and when I'd apply it on the IMAT?
DR. VENTUS — IMAT SCHOLAR
The Henderson-Hasselbalch equation — pH = pKa + log([A⁻]/[HA]) — describes the relationship between pH and the ratio of conjugate base to weak acid in a buffer system. On the IMAT, you'll encounter it in buffer calculation questions and acid-base physiology. The key insight is that when [A⁻] = [HA], pH = pKa exactly. For clinical application, blood pH of 7.4 with pKa of carbonic acid at 6.1 means your bicarbonate ratio must be precisely 20:1...
